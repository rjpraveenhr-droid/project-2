1984

Nineteen Eighty-Four (1984) is a seminal dystopian social science fiction novel written by English author George Orwell and published in 1949. The book serves as a terrifying warning against totalitarianism, state surveillance, and the manipulation of truth.

Cold Case Christianity

The Cold-Case Christianity book series, authored by real-life L.A. County cold-case homicide detective and former atheist J. Warner Wallace, applies rigorous criminal investigation frameworks to evaluate the historical reliability of Christian scripture.

DNA

The term "DNA Book" usually refers either to "DNA: The Story of the Genetic Revolution" by James D. Watson (co-discoverer of the double helix structure) or "The DNA Book" published by DK, which is a highly visual educational guide for children.

Project Hail Mary

Project Hail Mary is a critically acclaimed 2021 hard science fiction novel by Andy Weir (author of The Martian) that was adapted into a major sci-fi blockbuster movie starring Ryan Gosling in March 2026.The story follows Ryland Grace, a junior high school science teacher who wakes up with amnesia aboard an interstellar spaceship. As his memory piecemeal returns, he realizes he is the sole survivor of a desperate, last-chance mission to save humanity and Earth from an extinction-level solar crisis. To survive and succeed, he must rely on pure scientific logic, engineering ingenuity, and an incredibly unexpected alien ally nicknamed Rocky.

Lord Of The Rings

The Lord of the Rings is a legendary high-fantasy epic written by English scholar J. R. R. Tolkien. Written in stages between 1937 and 1949, it stands as one of the best-selling and most influential literary works in history, with over 150 million copies sold.Initially conceived as a sequel to Tolkien's 1937 children's book The Hobbit, it evolved into a massive, multi-layered mythos. Although frequently referred to as a trilogy, Tolkien structured it as a single unified novel divided into six "books", typically published across three individual volumes: The Fellowship of the Ring (1954), The Two Towers (1954), and The Return of the King (1955).

Psychology Of Money

The definitive book on this topic is The Psychology of Money by Morgan Housel, which explains that doing well with money is less about what you know and more about how you behave.

The Chronicles Of Narnia

C.S. Lewis wrote The Chronicles of Narnia because of a fascinating mix of mental images, real-life wartime experiences, and a desire to reimagine Christian theology for children.

The Truth In True Crime

Before writing bestselling books, J. Warner Wallace was a committed, self-described "angry atheist" for the first 35 years of his life. He served as a highly decorated Los Angeles County cold-case homicide detective, specializing in solving murders that had gone unsolved for decades.

War and Peace

War and Peace is a monumental epic historical masterpiece written by Russian author Leo Tolstoy and published in its entirety in 1869. Widely regarded as one of the greatest novels in world literature, the book weaves a sprawling fictional narrative with extensive philosophical essays challenging how history is written and understood.

The History Of World War II

The book featured on this cover, History of World War II: The Campaigns, Battles and Weapons from 1939 to 1945, is a direct reflection of its author's deep military expertise. Written and edited by renowned British military historian Dr. Chris McNab, this 448-page reference volume serves as a comprehensive, highly visual field guide to the 20th century's greatest global conflict.
